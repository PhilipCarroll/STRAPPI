Jaeger LeCoultre Club Automatic. Vintage Excellence.

The Jaeger-LeCoultre Club line was introduced in the '70s as an entry-level model. There were various styles produced, but this example is one of the most striking designs from the line, from the case to the dial. First, the case is wide and heavy, with the right balance of tonneau and square shapes to add serious presence to the watch. What’s neat about the dial is the the late '60s and '70s style with the chunky indexes and checkered racing-style outer track.

Classic Nato Strap. Military Inspired Watch Strap.

One of the most common mistakes watch enthusiasts and admirers make is assuming the NATO strap takes its name from the North Atlantic Treaty Organization. However, NATO is just the nickname given to a watch strap that’s actually called the G10. It's roots come from the British Ministry of Defence Standard in 1973 where the now-called NATO straps would be issued to soldiers who requested one.

Stowa Antea. Classic German Bauhaus Design

Stowa was one of the five watchmakers that supplied watches to the German Air Force, or Luftwaffe, during WWII alongside A. Lange & Söhne, IWC, Laco, and Wempe. The Baumuster “A” dial, which features Arabic hour numerals and a distinctive triangle and dots at 12, was originally produced in 1940 with a massive 55mm case and a Unitas 2812 movement. Stowa, however, didn’t recreate the flieger until the watch was resurrected in 1997. Since then, it’s become a classic among watch collectors.
